Corporate workshops for fast behavior change

If your last corporate training changed nothing, it was not the vendor's fault. It operated at the wrong level of the brain.

The problem is not that the team did not listen. The training entered through the wrong layer: information placed on top of intact beliefs. The path here is tactical: identify the inherited frame, make it visible in behavior, attach real arithmetic, and close with decisions that can be executed in seven days.

Most corporate programs make the same mistake: they teach new skills on top of beliefs that were never examined. The participant practices the technique for two weeks, the environment subtly punishes it, and the old operating system takes back control. Not for lack of willpower — for lack of intervention at the right layer.

The correct sequence does not start with theory. It starts with a recognizable crack: “this thing I do every month with my money” or “this thing I assume every day about my company.” Only then does the framework make sense: four movements in order — boundary, behavior, intellect and decision. Break the order and change turns back into talk.

The two workshops below work on two domains where that crack appears quickly: personal money and private enterprise. They can stand alone. Taken together, participants discover something stronger: the pattern sabotaging a financial decision is often the same pattern sabotaging their relationship with the company. That is not preached. It is made visible.

What the company buys

Not a polished talk. A two-hour intervention.

As an economic and business researcher, trainer and speaker, J.F. Carpio designs the session so participants do not merely “understand” an idea. They see it operating inside their own habits, numbers and work decisions.

  1. A diagnosis that does not humiliate.People lower their guard because they are not accused. They are shown the system they were using without seeing it.

Workshop 1 · 2 hours · Standalone

Money & Personal Finance

Participants arrive with an inherited financial operating system: beliefs formed before their first paycheck and never seriously examined.

  1. Boundary Draw the real map. The first move is not confrontational; it is cartographic. Is debt always bad? Is saving enough? What is a real asset? Honest diagnosis before any prescription.
  2. Behavior Make the patterns legible. The spending-debt-stress cycle, postponed decisions, credit used as an extension of income — made visible not as moral flaws, but as logical consequences of an operating system nobody handed to the participant with instructions.
  3. Intellect Deliver the tools. Simplified personal balance sheet, real cost of consumer debt, the distinction between active and passive income, the logic of an asset that works while the employee sleeps. Arithmetic applied to the participant's own numbers.
  4. Decision Concrete commitments with dates. The workshop closes with two or three specific decisions per participant — not abstract goals, but actions with a date and a number to execute in the next seven days.

Format — 110 min workshop with live exercises · 10 min Q&A.

Optional material — brief readings, videos and surgical homework at the end, left to each participant's discretion. Nothing long, nothing generic.

Workshop 2 · 2 hours · Standalone

Appreciation of Private Enterprise

The inherited operating system, in most cases, is a win-lose frame: the employer wins because the employee loses. It arrives before the contract. It is almost never examined with real arithmetic.

  1. Boundary From win-lose to win-win. The line is redrawn between the inherited story of "how companies work" and what the Mengerian line makes visible: employment exists precisely because both sides gain. The new boundary is not political — it is arithmetic.
  2. Behavior See the frame in action. Discretionary effort withheld, company assets treated as alien, information not shared, loyalty disappearing at the first tension. Visible as logical consequences of an incomplete model, not as character defects.
  3. Intellect The complete ledger. Each participant builds their employment P&L: salary as a guaranteed advance on future production, risk capital absorbed by the employer, productivity multiplied by infrastructure an independent worker could not replicate. Reisman and Say provide the arithmetic; the participant applies it to their own numbers.
  4. Decision Different frame, different decisions. The employee who understands private enterprise as a win-win mechanism treats assets differently, shares information differently, responds to adversity differently. That is what changes.

Format — 110 min workshop with live exercises · 10 min Q&A.

Optional material — brief readings, videos and surgical homework at the end, left to each participant's discretion. Nothing long, nothing generic.

Real transformation does not happen because the participant reads a point and thinks "that makes sense." It happens because during the workshop they discover those points in their own numbers, with their own calculator, in real time. Their salary, their company, their money, their future — all of it works differently from what they were taught. And that does not fade because it was not a motivational slogan. It was arithmetic.
