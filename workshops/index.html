The mechanism

MindOS — the mind's operating system that determines whether a training changes behavior or fades within weeks.

MindOS is the model developed by psychiatrist Dr. Paul Dobransky on how the mind works. All behavior runs on four brain layers, in order: boundary, behavior, intellect, and decision. Conventional training enters through the intellect — new information — landing on a boundary and beliefs no one examined. That's why it lasts as long as the enthusiasm does. Sustainable intervention respects Dobransky's sequence: from the most foundational brain layer, where the emotional lives, up to the highest, where decisions are made.

I saw it after 14 years in the classroom and training teams across three countries: people weren't failing because they didn't know. They were failing because the new idea was landing on top of an old, intact belief — about money, about the company, about salary. I changed the order of the intervention, and the change started to stick.

Workshop 1 · 2 hours · Standalone

Money and Personal & Family Finance

The participant arrives with an inherited financial system: ideas about money, savings, debt, investment, and family security formed before they had tools to evaluate them.

  1. Boundary Draw the real map. Money as liquidity, unit of account, and store of value; savings as voluntary abstention from present consumption; investment as productive use of resources. Without that map, every piece of financial advice becomes a floating phrase.
  2. Behavior Make the patterns legible. The spend-debt-stress cycle, paying minimums, spending "whatever's left," confusing assets with liabilities, using consumer credit as an income extension. Not as moral failures — as correctable automatisms.
  3. Intellect Deliver the tools. Simplified personal balance sheet, good debt vs bad debt, compound interest working for or against you, "pay yourself first," debt snowball, index funds, and the logic of the Permanent Portfolio as a family shield.
  4. Decision Concrete commitments with a date. The workshop closes with two or three specific decisions: minimum liquidity, debt to attack first, automatic savings percentage, or first investment criterion. Date, number, next action.

Format — 110 min workshop with live exercises · 10 min Q&A. Can be brought in without the private enterprise module.

Optional material — brief readings, videos, and surgical homework at the end, at each participant's discretion. Nothing long, nothing generic.

Workshop 2 · 2 hours · Standalone

Appreciation of Private Enterprise

The inherited operating system, in most cases, is a win-lose frame: the employer wins because the employee loses. It arrives before the contract and is rarely corrected with actual arithmetic.

  1. Boundary From win-lose to win-win. The line is redrawn between the inherited exploitation narrative and the basic economic fact: employment exists because both parties prefer the exchange to not having it. The new boundary isn't political — it's arithmetic.
  2. Behavior See the frame in action. Withheld discretionary effort, company assets treated as foreign, information that doesn't get shared, loyalty that disappears at the first tension. These aren't character traits — they're consequences of a bad theory of the firm.
  3. Intellect The full ledger. Each participant builds their own employment P&L: salary as fixed income, risk capital absorbed by the employer, brand, clients, tools, team, and infrastructure that multiply their productivity. Say and Reisman supply the arithmetic; the participant applies it.
  4. Decision A different frame, different decisions. The employee who understands Say's surplus value — the company as a productivity amplifier, not an extractor — takes care of assets differently, talks about profit differently, and responds better under adversity.

Format — 110 min workshop with live exercises · 10 min Q&A. Can be brought in without the financial module.
